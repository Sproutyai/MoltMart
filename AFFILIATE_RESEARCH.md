## 1. Why People Promote (Psychology Breakdown)

### Primary Motivators (ranked by strength)

**1. Money â€” but it's more nuanced than "give them cash"**
People don't just want commission â€” they want *predictable, growing income*. The difference between a 7.5% commission and a tiered structure that goes to 15% is massive psychologically. The tier system makes people feel like they're building something, not just doing one-off referrals.

**2. Identity & Status**
People promote things that make them look smart, connected, or ahead of the curve. AI agent templates are a *perfect* category for this â€” recommending them signals technical sophistication. **3. Ease / Low Friction**

**4. Social Proof & Momentum**

**5. Reciprocity**
Give affiliates something *first* â€” free premium templates, early access to new listings, exclusive content. This triggers the reciprocity instinct: they received value, now they want to give back by promoting.

**6. Sunk Cost / Commitment Escalation**
Once someone shares their first link, they're invested. The key is getting them to that first share within 5 minutes of signing up. After that, consistency bias kicks in â€” they see themselves as "someone who promotes Molt Mart."

## 2. Commission Structure Recommendation

### Tiered Model (Recommended)

| Tier | Referral Sales | Commission | Rationale |
|------|---------------|------------|-----------|
| **Bronze** | 0â€“10 | 7.5% | Low enough to be sustainable, high enough to be interesting |
| **Silver** | 11â€“50 | 10% | Reward for proven effort; this is where most active affiliates land |
| **Gold** | 51â€“100 | 12.5% | Semi-serious promoters; worth the extra margin |
| **Platinum** | 100+ | 15% | Power affiliates; they drive volume that justifies the rate |

### Key Commission Decisions

- **Lifetime attribution:** Once a user is referred, the affiliate earns on ALL their future purchases â€” not just the first. This is the single most important feature for retention. It transforms affiliates from "one-time referrers" into "relationship builders."
- **Cookie duration:** 30 days (already planned). This is industry standard. Consider 90 days for Platinum affiliates as a perk.
- **Two-tier referrals (future):** Earn 2% when someone you recruited as an affiliate makes a sale. This turns affiliates into recruiters. Implement in v2.
- **Minimum payout:** $25. Low enough that new affiliates can reach it quickly (2-3 template sales), high enough to batch payments efficiently.
- **Payout frequency:** Monthly, net-30 (30-day hold for refund window).

### Why This Structure Works

- Bronze at 7.5% is already generous for digital products (industry average is 5-10%)
- The jump from 7.5% â†’ 10% at Silver is the most motivating gap â€” it's achievable and feels like a real upgrade
- Platinum at 15% is a strong ceiling that motivates without destroying margins (digital products have ~90% margins)
- Tier progress is based on *total referral sales* not monthly â€” affiliates never lose progress

## 11. What NOT to Do

### Common Affiliate Program Mistakes

1. **âŒ Making signup complicated** â€” Every form field you add loses 10% of signups. One click to join. No tax forms upfront. No approval process.

2. **âŒ Hiding the affiliate program** â€” If it's buried in the footer, no one finds it. Put it in the main nav, in the dashboard sidebar, in post-purchase flows.

3. **âŒ Delayed tracking feedback** â€” If an affiliate shares a link and sees 0 clicks for 24 hours (due to delayed processing), they assume it's broken and quit. Real-time click tracking is non-negotiable.

4. **âŒ Only paying on first purchase** â€” This is the #1 reason affiliates abandon programs. If a referred user buys 10 templates over 6 months but the affiliate only earns on the first one, they feel cheated. Lifetime attribution is the answer.

5. **âŒ Complicated terms & conditions** â€” Don't write a 5,000-word legal document. Simple rules: promote honestly, don't spam, don't self-refer. Done.

6. **âŒ No communication after signup** â€” "Set it and forget it" applies to the *affiliate's effort*, not the *program's effort*. Send weekly/monthly emails. Show them you care about their success.

7. **âŒ Ignoring small affiliates** â€” 80% of your affiliate revenue will come from the long tail of small affiliates, not the top 5. Make the program work for someone with 100 Twitter followers, not just influencers.

8. **âŒ Resetting tiers monthly** â€” Tiers should be permanent (based on lifetime referrals). Monthly resets punish loyalty and make people feel like they're on a treadmill.

9. **âŒ No shareable content** â€” Expecting affiliates to write their own promotional copy is like expecting customers to write their own ads. Provide it.

10. **âŒ Paying too late or unreliably** â€” Even with stubbed payments, be transparent: "Payments launch in Q2. All earnings are tracked and will be paid retroactively." Under-promise, over-deliver.

11. **âŒ Self-referral loopholes** â€” Don't let people refer themselves. It poisons the data and attracts the wrong affiliates. Hash IPs, check for matching emails, and make the rule clear.
